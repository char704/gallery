# Product

## Register

product

## Users

FrameHub is for casual photo sharers who want a calm place to upload, organize, browse, and revisit personal photo collections. They may be sharing public snapshots with a small community, keeping private work sorted into albums, or searching for photos by tag without the pressure and noise of a social feed.

## Product Purpose

FrameHub helps people manage visual collections with clear privacy controls, album organization, public discovery, search, likes, and comments. Success means users can quickly upload a meaningful image, understand whether it is private or public, find it later, and move between browsing, viewing, commenting, and album management without friction.

## Brand Personality

Visual, calm, personal. The product should feel modern and focused, with the interface supporting the photos rather than competing with them. The tone is welcoming and direct: useful enough for repeated organization work, but soft enough to feel like a personal gallery.

## Anti-references

FrameHub should not feel like an Instagram clone, a generic social media feed, or a flashy portfolio template. Avoid excessive gradients, oversized animations, cluttered cards, generic SaaS dashboard styling, engagement-first dark patterns, and decorative UI that competes with the photographs.

## Design Principles

- Let photos lead: image content is the primary visual material, and chrome should stay quiet around it.
- Make privacy unmistakable: public, private, and unlisted states need clear labels, icons, and copy rather than color alone.
- Keep gallery work low-friction: browsing, searching, opening an image, uploading, commenting, and managing albums should stay direct and predictable.
- Favor personal calm over social noise: engagement is supported, but it must not become the organizing principle of the interface.
- Preserve trust through consistency: navigation, forms, actions, loading, error, and empty states should share one component vocabulary.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA where practical. Support full keyboard navigation, visible focus states, semantic HTML, sufficient color contrast, accessible form labels, and descriptive alt text for meaningful images. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Do not rely on color alone for privacy states, errors, tags, or status indicators. Core workflows including browsing photos, searching, opening an image, uploading, commenting, and managing albums should remain usable with a keyboard.
